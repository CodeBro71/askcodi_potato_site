The Great Irish Potato Famine

Overview

The Great Irish Potato Famine, also known as the Great Hunger, was a period of mass starvation and disease in Ireland from 1845 to 1852.

Causes

The main cause of the famine was the failure of the potato crop, which was the primary food source for the Irish population.

Impact

The famine resulted in the deaths of over 1 million people and the emigration of 1 million more.
