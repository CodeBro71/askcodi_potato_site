Irish Potato Famine of the 19th Century

Overview

The Irish Potato Famine was a period of mass starvation and disease in Ireland from 1845 to 1852. It was caused by a potato blight that destroyed the potato crops, which were the main food source for the Irish population at the time.

Causes

The main cause of the famine was the Phytophthora infestans, a fungus-like organism that attacked the potato crops and led to their failure. The reliance on potatoes as a staple food source, the oppressive British colonial rule, and the lack of proper food distribution also contributed to the severity of the famine.

Impact

The Irish Potato Famine resulted in the deaths of over a million people and the emigration of another million. It led to widespread poverty, disease, and social upheaval in Ireland, with long-lasting effects on the country's population and economy.
